Quiet Moon

A minimal, public-domain plan for a 42-day NICU quality-improvement pilot: calmer sensory environments and more skin-to-skin — while oxygen targets remain exactly per unit policy.

Why this exists

Preterm brain development is sensitive to sensory load (noise, light, frequent alarms). Skin-to-skin (“kangaroo care”) helps physiologic regulation. Quiet Moon asks a narrow question: does a simple, policy-compliant bundle help infants spend more time within your unit’s SpO₂ range and have fewer instability events?

No oxygen experiments De-identified, QI-style observation One page + local logger

Scope (what you do)

Environment

  • Track noise (dBA), light (lux), and alarm counts per room/day.
  • Introduce quiet hours, dim lights at night, and reduce non-actionable alarms (per policy).

Care Exposure

  • Capture daily skin-to-skin minutes from routine charting.
  • Offer simple family checklists to support steady minutes.

Measures & Simple Analysis

Primary

  • % time SpO₂ in unit range (your pathway defines the range).
  • Desaturation/bradycardia events per 24h.

Secondary

  • Skin-to-skin minutes/day; noise, light, alarm counts.
  • Optional: HRV, sleep state minutes, PIPP scores.

42-Day cadence

  1. Weeks 1–2: Baseline observation.
  2. Weeks 3–4: Bundle on (quiet hours, alarm hygiene, coaching).
  3. Weeks 5–6: Sustain & summarize (one-page memo).

Explainable: Local Logger

The logger is a simple, offline tool that lives at /illumina1/logger.html. It stores entries in the browser’s LocalStorage on the device in use; nothing is uploaded or transmitted.

What it records

  • Day (1–42), date, and phase (baseline / bundle / sustain).
  • SpO₂ time-in-range %, desat/brady events per 24h.
  • Skin-to-skin minutes, noise (dBA), light (lux), alarm counts.

What it does not record

  • No names, MRNs, dates of birth, or room numbers.
  • No IP collection, cookies, analytics, or network requests.

Why local-only?

Local storage keeps testing DIY and privacy-preserving. Teams can export CSV/JSON, review offline, and—if approved—merge into their secure systems.

Safety Notes

  • Clinical care unchanged: SpO₂ target ranges remain exactly per your unit pathway.
  • Minimal-risk QI intent: observational pre/post; environment and exposure changes are policy-compliant.
  • De-identification required: aggregate logs only; no protected health information (PHI) enters the page or logger.
  • Pause rule: if instability events rise for ≥3 consecutive days, stop and review with the neonatologist/RT lead.
  • IRB/QI check: confirm local requirements before starting.

Invitation for Peer Review

Quiet Moon is intentionally small. If you’re a clinician, researcher, or parent partner, we welcome critique and improvements.

How to review (short checklist)

  • Are the in-range and event measures well defined for your unit?
  • Is the environment bundle feasible without changing clinical pathways?
  • Would any fields be mis-interpreted by staff or families?
  • What confounders (GA, weight, respiratory support) should be annotated in the memo?

Starter References & Reading

  • American Academy of Pediatrics guidance on NICU environmental noise/light and developmental care.
